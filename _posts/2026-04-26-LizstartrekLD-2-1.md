Every Sunday my Twitch subs and I watch Star Trek in my Discord. Here are my reviews and thoughts on each of the episodes as I see it. If you're curious about a nerd's views on Star Trek episodes, please read on, I go quite in depth with some of these episodes! I've watched some Star Trek before but not all of it!

I know very little about this show except it was made by nerds like me with a very different sense of humor. God, please be good.


**Strange Energies** - ENT
4/10

<img src="https://imgur.com/DXbpcFH.png" alt="trek">

Our season starts with Mariner busting out of a Cardassian torture center, while Mariner is expositing about how her life is exhausting. This SHOW is exhausting. I was hoping they'd slow down a little, but no, still going a mile a minute. This is a hologram btw, we aren't like at war with the Cardassians, I hope.

Mariner and her mom are getting along a lot better, and it's rubbing the rest of the crew the wrong way. Our B plot is Tendi and Rutherford, again. Rutherford lost his memory and he's a bit different. Tendi is worried he has some kind of deadly condition that effects cyborgs.

<img src="https://imgur.com/HNkVWIz.png" alt="trek">

Mariner does a power washing on some ancient monuments that unleashes an energy wave that hits Ransom. He becomes a superpowered god and goes mad with power. As annoying as I find this plot point, it's classic Trek and we haven't seen it in a while.

<img src="https://imgur.com/GkPtyWq.png" alt="trek">

Ransom becomes a giant head and tries to eat the ship. They need to short out his godliness by making him more powerful. Meanwhile, Tendi chases Rutherford around the ship with guns and stuff. She eventually admits its a lie and just wanted to cut into his brain to make sure he likes her. He finds it cool. That's weird as hell but okay, if they're good for each other.

They ignore Mariner's suggestion to overpower him, and try to talk him down instead. It doesn't work, so Mariner kicks him in the dick which depowers him. Okay. Mariner and her mom decide "okay maybe working together wasn't working, let's go back to the status quo". All's back to the status quo... except Boimler still isn't here. How's he doing on that ship with Riker anyway?

<img src="https://imgur.com/bN3cqg4.png" alt="trek">

Poorly! Why did they make Riker like this anyway, it's kinda weirding me out.


**Kayshon, His Eyes Open** - ENT
6/10

<img src="https://imgur.com/XQ7jAtW.png" alt="trek">

This episode introduces a few new characters. We need a replacement for Boimler, that's Jet Manhaver, he's a tightass who butts heads with Mariner a lot. And then we have the new security staff, that's Kayshon, a Tamarian. Remember them, from like, the best episode? Darmok indeed.

Meanwhile, Boimler is still having trouble settling in on the Titan. Neli is complaining about how the Titan is a science recon ship but all they show it in is combat missions. That's very Neli and while I don't care, I do care, y'know?

Riker's crew is like "man, the Enterprise D was so lame and boring". It's like it was made directly to annoy my chat lol

<img src="https://imgur.com/sBIGXbJ.png" alt="trek">

The Lower Deckers are going to a guy's collection ship to reposses his stuff after he died. This sets off his security, and Kayshon is turned into a doll. Poor guy. Mariner and Jet are arguing a lot and fighting the entire time.

Riker's team are infiltrating some Pakled. One of them is like "We got a 'led over there". DON'T SAY IT LIKE THAT. WE DON'T NEED TO INVENT SLURS. Riker's crew is like "THIS IS WHAT STARFLEET IS ALL ABOUT, FIGHTING TO THE DEATH IN PHASER FIGHTS" and Boimler finally is like "What are you talking about? Starfleet is about exploring and boldly goingly, that's what Starfleet is to me." The others go "Oh yeah, actually, I got into Starfleet to study moss." This is NOT how I expected this to go. That's not the vibe of Lower Decks.

They eventually realize that NEITHER Mariner OR Jet should be in charge, and Tendi and Rutherford fix the problems very quickly.

<img src="https://imgur.com/MCiBPSa.png" alt="trek">

Boimler gets cloned like Riker's clone did, and one of them gets sent back to the Cerritos. They IMMEDIATELY replace Jet with him, and all's well. Oh and Kayshon goes back to normal.

We find out that Mariner is pansexual. Cool. She even namedrops "bad nonbinaries". Sick.

Boimler goes nuts from a gasleak and Tom Paris tackles him.

Mariner and Tendi crash the shuttle into the ship. It bounces off the shields. ***THAT'S NOT HOW SHIELDS WORK.*** They only work on energy weapons. Okay.

The conclusion to the Rutherford plot is that he learns horrifying secrets of death and it makes him upset.

This epsiode was ALL over the place.

They're from a TOS episode.

Meanwhile, we got another B plot. Tendi needs to scan some people who are avoiding it so she can impress the doctor. She is bad at asserting herself.

<img src="https://imgur.com/x5EBsNX.png" alt="trek">

Also turns out that the last holdout for people avoiding seeing the doctor... is THE DOCTOR HERSELF! She chases her around the ship, it's fun. She breaks her arm, whoops.

<img src="https://imgur.com/GBmWdRG.png" alt="trek">

And also the jokes were pretty funny here. I like how it ends with them playing a board game they both love, and though MAriner barely understands it, she's kicking their teeth in.

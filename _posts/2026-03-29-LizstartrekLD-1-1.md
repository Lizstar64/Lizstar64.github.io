Every Sunday my Twitch subs and I watch Star Trek in my Discord. Here are my reviews and thoughts on each of the episodes as I see it. If you're curious about a nerd's views on Star Trek episodes, please read on, I go quite in depth with some of these episodes! I've watched some Star Trek before but not all of it!

**Second Contact** - ENT
3.5/10

<img src="https://imgur.com/pfLbgkS.png" alt="trek">

We're introduced very quickly to two Ensigns. Boimler and Mariner. Boimler is high strung, lame, and has illusions of grandeur, making a fake captains log. Mariner is a kinda shitty killjoy who has EXTREME illusions of grandeur. Mariner injurs Boimler with a batleth while drunk on Romulan Ale. Okay, next scene!

We're now introduced to Ensign Tendi, an Orion who's very bright eyed and bushy tailed, but y'know, she's lower decks so she'll be getting beat down over time I'm sure. They also meet another ensign, Rutherford, an engineer who's a cyborg and ALSO a nervous wreck. There's a lot of that here. Hey, is this review fast and hard to follow without a lot of detail? Yes! That's HOW THIS SHOW IS INTRODUCED.

Everyone is talking so fucking fast, jokes are flung in your face every 5 seconds with no time to breath and then we move right on to the next joke or the next scene. If you speak REALLY fast everyone will think what you say is smart, except for Funny this time. It is... NOT easy to follow. And the jokes are pretty rough, IIRC. Lots of awkward humor, which is VERY not my speed.

<img src="https://imgur.com/wJMdMQe.png" alt="trek">

Boimler is told by her captain to keep an eye on Mariner, and he takes it way WAY too seriously and makes things really fucking weird. That's his character trait! The two of them scare off some aliens at the planet they're on and then get attacked by a spider that shoots webbing out of its butt. You might think that's weird for me to specify, but the show sure does! Ha ha, butts are funny!

<img src="https://imgur.com/2Oe80Sc.png" alt="trek">

Meanwhile, one of the bridge officers got infected with a zombie virus and its spreading violently through the ship. The show cuts from scenes of graphic zombie violence to Rutherford having a date. It's incongruent. That's the point and it's supposed to be funny.... okay, it's actually not bad. I kinda like this part. They're walking around "on a date walk" as they blast zombies and talk about the music they're into. Thats kinda cute. Unfortunately the date doesn't work cause Rutherford is kind of a huge dork.

<img src="https://imgur.com/BO01AXj.png" alt="trek">

Why are their clothes off? Don't worry about it.

Boimler gets covered in alien slime goo and is taken to sick bay cause it's a cure for the virus. All of the bridge crew ignore Boimler and his contributions and they are basically having their own Star Trek episode while the Lower Deckers are in the background. I think that's supposed to be the entire premise and conceit of the entire show but also I kinda hate it? It's like, really unpleasant to watch.

You gotta take this show with a BIT more of a grain of salt cause it's a hyperbolic joke show, but this is still not my favorite general conceit. And I'd LOVE a show about lower decks people.

Anyways, Boimler does NOT narc on Mariner, and they become "friends". Mariner is also the captain's daughter, who hates her and wants to throw her in the brig for no reason.

This was so fucking hard to follow and write a review of holy HELL. This was a very rough introduction, but I can see how there are sparks of something good here.
